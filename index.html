EMG2Tendon

Bridging EMG signals to Tendon Dynamics

About the Project

EMG2Tendon aims to decode tendon movement and control from surface EMG signals. Combining supervised learning with reinforcement learning, the project models the tendon dynamics (x2) from EMG (x1) to predict hand poses (y) effectively. A MuJoCo-based simulation is used to learn the tendon mechanics in a physics-consistent manner.

Why EMG2Tendon?

Traditional prosthetics often struggle with intuitive control. By directly mapping muscle signals to tendon behaviors, EMG2Tendon enables finer, more natural control of prosthetic and exoskeleton devices, opening up new possibilities for assistive technology and human augmentation.

Technical Approach

  • Supervised learning for EMG-to-hand pose prediction (x1 → y)
  • Reinforcement learning for tendon behavior modeling (x2 → y)
  • Joint optimization to infer tendon states x2 from EMG x1 without direct supervision
  • Simulation in MuJoCo for tendon physics validation
